# Marketing Department KPIs

## Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used to evaluate the effectiveness of the Marketing Department.

KPIs should support continuous learning, strategic decision-making and sustainable business growth.

---

# Guiding Principles

Marketing KPIs should be:

- strategically aligned
- measurable
- actionable
- explainable
- comparable over time
- connected to Business Return and Life Return

Marketing should optimize outcomes rather than activity alone.

---

# KPI Categories

## 1. Audience Growth

Measures expansion of the business audience.

Examples:

- Newsletter Subscribers
- Podcast Followers
- YouTube Subscribers
- LinkedIn Followers
- Pinterest Followers
- Website Visitors

---

## 2. Trust & Authority

Measures perceived expertise and credibility.

Examples:

- Returning Visitors
- Podcast Completion Rate
- Newsletter Open Rate
- Engagement Rate
- Shares
- Testimonials
- Backlinks

---

## 3. Lead Generation

Measures creation of qualified opportunities.

Examples:

- Lead Magnet Downloads
- Discovery Calls
- Qualified Leads
- Sponsorship Enquiries
- Contact Form Submissions

---

## 4. Content Performance

Measures effectiveness of marketing assets.

Examples:

- Episode Downloads
- Video Views
- Article Reads
- Social Engagement
- Average Watch Time

---

## 5. Business Impact

Measures contribution to commercial success.

Examples:

- Revenue Influenced
- Conversion Rate
- Customer Acquisition
- Sponsor Revenue
- Affiliate Revenue

---

## 6. Operational Excellence

Measures marketing efficiency.

Examples:

- Campaign Cycle Time
- Human Time Saved
- AI Utilization
- Automation Rate
- Reusable Assets Created

---

## 7. Strategic Health

Measures long-term sustainability.

Examples:

- Business Return Contribution
- Life Return Contribution
- Human Signature Protection
- Marketing Capability Score
- PBHS Marketing Capability Trend

---

# KPI Dashboard

The Marketing Department Dashboard should display:

- Overall Marketing Health Score
- Audience Growth
- Trust Score
- Lead Generation
- Business Return
- Life Return
- Active Campaigns
- AI Employee Utilization

---

# KPI Review Cadence

| Frequency | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Weekly | Operational review |
| Monthly | Department review |
| Quarterly | Executive Board review |
| Annually | Strategic evaluation |

---

# Thresholds

Each KPI should define:

- Target
- Warning Threshold
- Critical Threshold

PBOS should proactively identify declining trends before they become business problems.

---

# Executive Reporting

The Chief Marketing Officer reports:

- KPI trends
- campaign performance
- strategic risks
- improvement opportunities
- recommended investments

to the Executive Board.

---

# Relationship to PBHS

Marketing KPIs provide objective evidence for future PBHS assessments.

Performance data strengthens recommendation quality and Executive Board decision-making.

---

# Architectural Principle

Marketing success is measured by sustainable business outcomes rather than content volume.

Every KPI should support better executive decisions.
