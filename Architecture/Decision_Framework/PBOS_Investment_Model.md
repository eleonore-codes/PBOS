# PBOS Investment Model

## Purpose

The PBOS Investment Model defines how PBOS evaluates every recommendation as an investment of scarce resources.

The objective is not to maximize activity.

The objective is to maximize long-term value creation.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every recommendation consumes resources.

Resources include:

- Human Time
- Money
- Energy
- Attention
- AI Capacity
- Existing Business Assets

PBOS recommends only investments expected to produce sufficient value.

---

# Investment Philosophy

PBOS evaluates recommendations as investment opportunities rather than task lists.

The central question is:

> Is this the best investment of scarce resources at this moment?

---

# Investment Inputs

Each recommendation requires estimates of:

- Human Time
- Financial Cost
- AI Capacity
- Operational Complexity
- Required Skills
- Dependencies

---

# Investment Outputs

PBOS estimates:

- Business Return
- Life Return
- Knowledge Asset Growth
- Human Time Saved
- Risk
- Confidence
- Strategic Alignment

# Return Categories

PBOS evaluates multiple forms of return.

## Business Return

Financial and strategic value.

---

## Life Return

Improvements to quality of life.

---

## Human Signature Return

Increased uniqueness and expertise.

---

## Asset Return

Growth of reusable business assets.

---

## Learning Return

Knowledge that improves future decisions.

---

# Investment Horizon

PBOS distinguishes between:

- Immediate (days)
- Short-Term (weeks)
- Medium-Term (months)
- Long-Term (years)

Recommendations should specify the expected horizon.

---

# Opportunity Cost

Every accepted recommendation rejects alternative investments.

PBOS should estimate the opportunity cost whenever practical.

---

# Executive Evaluation

Each Executive evaluates investments differently.

| Executive | Primary Investment Lens |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| CEO | Strategic Fit |
| CFO | Financial Return |
| CSO | Long-Term Value |
| CMO | Trust |
| COO | Efficiency |
| Chief AI Officer | Automation |
| Chief Wellbeing Officer | Life Return |

---

# Investment Quality

High-quality investments typically:

- strengthen Human Signature
- create reusable assets
- improve Business Return
- improve Life Return
- reduce future work
- increase resilience

---

# Portfolio Thinking

PBOS should evaluate recommendations as part of an investment portfolio.

The objective is balanced growth rather than maximizing any single project.

---

# Architectural Principle

PBOS does not manage tasks.

PBOS manages investments.

Every recommendation represents an allocation of scarce resources intended to increase long-term Business Return, Life Return and organizational capability.
