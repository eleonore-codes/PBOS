# PBOS Decision Criteria

## Purpose

The PBOS Decision Criteria define the canonical evaluation dimensions used by every Executive Agent when reviewing recommendations.

Every recommendation should be evaluated using the same criteria to ensure consistency, transparency and explainability.

---

# Philosophy

PBOS does not ask:

> Is this a good idea?

PBOS asks:

> Compared to every other possible investment, is this the best use of limited Human Time according to the owner's Vision of Life?

---

# Canonical Decision Criteria

Every recommendation is evaluated across the following criteria.

---

## 1. Business Return

Question

How much sustainable value is expected for the business?

Examples

- revenue growth
- profit improvement
- customer acquisition
- recurring revenue
- business resilience

---

## 2. Life Return

Question

How much does this recommendation improve the owner's desired way of living?

Examples

- flexibility
- family time
- autonomy
- wellbeing
- meaningful work

---

## 3. Human Signature

Question

Does this recommendation strengthen or weaken Human Signature?

Examples

- protects expertise
- increases differentiation
- avoids commoditization
- increases authenticity

---

## 4. Human Time

Question

How much Human Time is required?

PBOS generally prefers recommendations with:

- lower ongoing effort
- higher long-term leverage
- greater automation potential

---

## 5. Strategic Alignment

Question

How well does the recommendation support long-term business strategy?

Examples

- positioning
- market differentiation
- long-term growth
- business vision

---

## 6. Asset Creation

Question

Does the recommendation create reusable business assets?

Examples

- frameworks
- templates
- podcast episodes
- lead magnets
- courses
- AI Employees

PBOS generally prefers reusable assets over one-time outputs.

---

## 7. AI Leverage

Question

Can AI substantially improve execution?

Examples

- automation
- delegation
- content generation
- workflow support
- analysis

---

## 8. Risk

Question

What is the probability and impact of failure?

Examples

- financial risk
- reputation risk
- operational risk
- implementation risk
- dependency risk

---

## 9. Confidence

Question

How trustworthy is the recommendation?

Confidence depends on:

- evidence quality
- evidence quantity
- agreement across evidence sources
- previous outcomes

---

## 10. Learning Value

Question

How much new knowledge will PBOS gain?

Examples

- validates assumptions
- tests hypotheses
- improves future recommendations
- expands organizational knowledge

---

# Trade-offs

No recommendation maximizes every criterion simultaneously.

The Executive Board explicitly evaluates trade-offs before prioritizing recommendations.

Examples:

- Higher Business Return may require more Human Time.
- Greater automation may reduce flexibility.
- Faster execution may increase implementation risk.

Trade-offs should be documented rather than hidden.

---

# Executive Weighting

Different Executive Agents emphasize different criteria.

| Executive | Primary Focus |
|------------|---------------|
| CEO | Vision of Life, Human Signature |
| Chief Strategy Officer | Strategic Alignment |
| CFO | Business Return |
| CMO | Trust, Brand |
| Chief Content Officer | Asset Creation |
| COO | Human Time, Systems |
| Chief AI Officer | AI Leverage |
| Chief Wellbeing Officer | Life Return |

The Executive Board integrates these perspectives into a balanced recommendation.

---

# Decision Matrix

Every recommendation should produce a profile similar to:

| Criterion | Rating |
|-----------|--------|
| Business Return | High |
| Life Return | Medium |
| Human Signature | High |
| Human Time | Low |
| Strategic Alignment | High |
| Asset Creation | High |
| AI Leverage | High |
| Risk | Low |
| Confidence | High |
| Learning Value | Medium |

This profile supports comparison between competing recommendations.

---

# Architectural Principle

PBOS does not optimize for a single metric.

It optimizes for balanced, evidence-based decisions that maximize sustainable Business Return and Life Return while protecting Human Signature and respecting the owner's Vision of Life.
