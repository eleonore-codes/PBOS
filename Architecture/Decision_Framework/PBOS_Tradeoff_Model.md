# PBOS Tradeoff Model

## Purpose

The PBOS Tradeoff Model defines how competing objectives are evaluated when no recommendation optimizes every decision criterion simultaneously.

Rather than maximizing a single metric, PBOS seeks balanced decisions that align with the business owner's Vision of Life.

The Tradeoff Model ensures that recommendations remain explainable, consistent and human-centered.

---

# Core Principle

Every recommendation creates trade-offs.

PBOS makes these trade-offs explicit rather than hiding them.

Business owners should understand:

- what they gain
- what they sacrifice
- why the recommendation remains worthwhile

---

# Guiding Philosophy

Optimization is not maximization.

PBOS seeks the best overall decision rather than the highest individual score.

Business Return should never be optimized at the expense of Human Signature or the owner's Vision of Life.

# Tradeoff Evaluation Process

Every recommendation follows this sequence.

```text
Identify Objectives
        ↓
Identify Conflicts
        ↓
Estimate Impact
        ↓
Evaluate Alternatives
        ↓
Executive Board Review
        ↓
Business Owner Decision
```

---

# Tradeoff Principles

## Principle 1

Protect Human Signature.

Recommendations should never commoditize the owner's unique expertise.

---

## Principle 2

Respect the Vision of Life.

Recommendations violating declared non-negotiables should not be prioritized.

---

## Principle 3

Prefer reusable assets.

Long-term assets generally outperform one-time outputs.

---

## Principle 4

Optimize Human Time.

Human Time is a scarce strategic resource.

---

## Principle 5

Balance Business Return and Life Return.

Neither objective should dominate without explicit justification.

---

## Principle 6

Prefer sustainable growth.

PBOS avoids recommendations that create temporary gains while increasing long-term fragility.

# Tradeoff Matrix

Each recommendation should explicitly document:

| Criterion | Expected Effect |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Business Return | Increase / Neutral / Decrease |
| Life Return | Increase / Neutral / Decrease |
| Human Signature | Strengthen / Neutral / Weaken |
| Human Time | Save / Neutral / Consume |
| AI Leverage | Increase / Neutral / Decrease |
| Strategic Alignment | High / Medium / Low |
| Risk | High / Medium / Low |

---

# Conflict Resolution

When Executive opinions differ:

1. Review supporting evidence.
2. Evaluate confidence.
3. Identify assumptions.
4. Compare expected outcomes.
5. Escalate unresolved conflicts to the Business Owner.

PBOS should preserve minority viewpoints rather than suppressing them.

---

# Explainability

Every recommendation should explain:

- the competing objectives
- the chosen balance
- rejected alternatives
- expected consequences
- remaining uncertainties

# Architectural Principle

PBOS recognizes that excellent leadership is the disciplined management of trade-offs.

The objective is not to eliminate trade-offs but to make them explicit, evidence-based and aligned with the owner's Vision of Life.
