# PBOS Strategic Initiatives

## Purpose

Strategic Initiatives transform Strategic Objectives into coordinated programs of work.

An initiative groups multiple related projects that together achieve a strategic outcome.

Initiatives bridge executive planning and operational execution.

---

# Mission

Coordinate investments so that projects reinforce one another rather than compete for resources.

PBOS executes initiatives—not isolated projects.

---

# Definition

A Strategic Initiative is a coordinated collection of projects that advances one Strategic Objective.

Initiatives have:

- business purpose
- executive sponsor
- measurable outcomes
- defined scope
- planning horizon
- portfolio priority

# Initiative Structure

Every initiative contains:

- Initiative ID
- Title
- Description
- Strategic Objective
- Executive Sponsor
- Business Case
- Expected Business Return
- Expected Life Return
- Success Metrics
- Related Projects
- Dependencies
- Status

# Initiative Governance

The Executive Board:

- approves initiatives
- allocates resources
- prioritizes execution
- reviews progress
- retires completed initiatives

The Business Owner approves final execution.

---

# Dependencies

An initiative may depend upon:

- another initiative
- PBHS capability improvements
- AI Employee availability
- budget approval
- completed projects

Dependencies should be explicit.

---

# Success Metrics

Each initiative should define:

- completion rate
- Business Return
- Life Return
- capability improvements
- reusable assets created
- Human Time saved
- AI leverage achieved

---

# Relationship to Operational AI Employees

Operational AI Employees execute projects belonging to initiatives.

They do not create initiatives.

Initiatives are created by the Executive Board.

---

# Architectural Principle

Initiatives organize investments.

Projects organize work.

PBOS optimizes coordinated initiatives rather than isolated activities.
