# PBHS Question Bank

## Purpose

The PBHS Question Bank defines the standardized assessment items used to evaluate the core business capabilities within PBHS.

Questions are organized by capability rather than by assessment form.

Future versions may present different subsets of questions depending on the business owner, industry, maturity level or previous assessment results.

The Question Bank represents only one evidence source within the broader PBHS Evidence Model.

---

# Design Principles

Questions should be:

- capability-oriented
- evidence-based
- actionable
- easy to understand
- measurable
- suitable for repeated assessments
- free from unnecessary jargon

Questions should measure business capabilities rather than business outcomes.

---

# Response Scale

Unless otherwise specified, every item uses the following five-point Likert scale.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------:|---------|
| 1 | Strongly Disagree |
| 2 | Disagree |
| 3 | Neither Agree nor Disagree |
| 4 | Agree |
| 5 | Strongly Agree |

# Capability 1 — Human Signature

## Construct

The owner's unique expertise, judgment and authenticity.

### Measurement Goal

Evaluate whether the business is built around capabilities that cannot easily be copied or automated.

### Questions

**HS-01**

My business clearly communicates expertise that competitors cannot easily copy.

**HS-02**

Customers primarily choose my business because of my unique knowledge, experience or perspective.

**HS-03**

Most of my weekly work focuses on activities that only I can perform.

**HS-04**

I actively protect my Human Signature from routine operational work.

**HS-05**

My promotional content authentically reflects my expertise and values.

# Question Metadata

Every question should maintain the following metadata:

- Question ID
- Capability
- Construct
- Measurement Goal
- Response Scale
- Version
- Date Introduced
- Status (Active / Experimental / Retired)

This enables future versioning without losing historical comparability.

---

# Adaptive Assessments

Future versions of PBHS may select questions dynamically based on:

- previous responses
- business stage
- industry
- available evidence
- AI interview results
- longitudinal trends

The Question Bank therefore serves as a repository rather than a fixed questionnaire.

---

# Architectural Principle

Questions are measurement instruments.

Business decisions are generated from the interpretation of capabilities supported by multiple sources of evidence.

The Question Bank therefore complements—but never replaces—the broader PBHS Evidence Model.
